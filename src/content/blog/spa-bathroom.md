The bathroom is probably my favourite room. In the morning this is where I get ready for my day and in the evening this is where I wash off the frenzy of my day. This is so important for my well being, physically and psychologically.

When I designed my own bathroom decor in 2014, I already wanted it to feel like a spa. And I want my bathroom design and my clients' bathroom interior to feel like a haven of peace — a relaxing and intimate room, just like a spa would be. Investing in your bathroom is investing in your wellness and happiness.

## Serene colour scheme

When thinking of a spa I have in mind soothing colours, gentle to the eye, nothing too bright. Earthy tones are always a good choice — inspire yourself from nature: green shades, soft orange and browns, or beige and greys. Keep your colour scheme harmonious and create an effortless looking space to let your mind and body unwind.

## Using natural furnishing

Using natural materials for your spa bathroom will strengthen that relaxed feel. Think about Scandinavian saunas with natural wood, or Moroccan spas with waterproof lime plaster. All is natural to the eye and to the touch, strengthening the soothing aspect of your bathroom interior.

## The right shower and bathtub

If you can, having a bathtub is wonderful for those times when you want to relax and unwind in a bubble bath. Personally I am a big fan of whirlpool bathtubs. You could also turn your shower room into a steam shower or hammam — with a waterproofed enclosure, it is quite easy to install and more affordable than you think.

## Mood lighting

For the spa feel, consider ambient and accent lights alongside task lighting. Soften natural light where needed, and make sure you can dim your general lights. Accent lighting can enhance art, plants, or interesting features — and candles always help.

## Soft and luxury linen

Choose soft, fluffy towels — buying in store for a first purchase helps you judge quality. Installing a heated towel rail in a practical spot will make winter feel comfortably warm and cosy.

## Tidy up

Plenty of storage behind closed cabinet doors keeps the room decluttered. An organised bathroom goes a very long way in maintaining a feeling of calm.

## Spa music and scent

A bluetooth speaker makes spa music easy. Fragrance sticks, scented oils, and candles add an olfactory layer that is often neglected but makes a real difference.
